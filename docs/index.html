We will implement an interactive solar system simulation system, which could simulate from simple celestial body motion to complex motions like collision and potentially the Three Body Problem.


Team Member

Guowei Yang, Jia Ze Yu, Yao Fu

Background

Sci-Fi movies have been more and more popular nowadays, and lots of Sci-Fi movies are related to space travel, for example, Interstellar, Star Wars, and even The Avengers: Endgame. Those movies has compelling space scenes that are extremely realistic.


Simulating the pure physics of celestial bodies aren’t complicated, but in order to make the celestial bodies themselves realistic, we also need to map planet textures onto the planet objects. Also, for example, in the Solar System, the sun is casting light on all the surrounding planets, therefore we have to render the shadows on the planets as well. Moreover, celestial bodies might collide due to their intersecting orbits. We would like to render the process of collisions as well.

Goals and Deliverables

We plan to deliver a renderer that is able to generate a short clip of simulated collisions between planets of different sizes, densities, colors, hardness and textures based on the laws of physics. We also hope to achieve different imaginary planetary collision effects including shattering and gooey collisions. We hope to make these effects as photo-realistic as possible by adding ray tracing.



Baseline Goals

We plan to model the planets as rigid spherical bodies of particles which shatter upon collision and their motion are dependent upon their respective forces of gravity with one another. Computing the position of each particle according to the laws of physics in a reasonable runtime will be challenging so we plan to explore techniques, data structures and machine learning algorithms that can help us accelerate this computation. The result will probably look something like this. We plan to evaluate the performance of our renderer by comparing our results with the planet collisions rendered in popular sci-fi movies and documentaries.




To further render planet collisions realistically, we need to consider the lighting, texture and the geological composition of the planet as well. We hope to apply ray tracing such that we can properly simulate the light emitted when two planets collide with each other. We also hope to simulate how the magma becomes visible on the surfaces of the planets as they approach each other due to their respective gravities.

Aspirational Goals

If we get ahead of schedule, we want to render collision between stars which are more difficult to model due to their complex physical properties and lighting conditions. We also want to explore hypothetical situations such as the collision of two gooey planets because we believe that computer graphics not only helps us render a model of reality, it also allows us to transcend reality and render the imaginary.
